# Behind The Foundry

How The Foundry thinks, decides, and builds your training program.

---

## How Onboarding Shapes Your Program

When you first open The Foundry, you answer three questions. Each one changes how your entire training program is built.

### Experience Level

Your experience level controls two things: which exercises you see and how many sets you start with.

Every exercise in The Foundry's database has a difficulty rating from 1 to 3. When you select your experience, the app filters exercises so you only see ones you can handle safely.

| Experience | Max Difficulty | What This Means |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner | 1 | Basic compound movements and machines. No advanced techniques. |
| Intermediate | 2 | More exercise variety. Cable work, dumbbell variations, moderate complexity. |
| Advanced | 3 | Full exercise library unlocked. Olympic lifts, advanced isolation work, everything. |

Experience also affects how weight increases are suggested week to week (more on that in the Carryover section).

### Primary Goal

Your goal changes the rep ranges for every exercise in your program. This is based on exercise science — different rep ranges train your muscles in different ways.

The Foundry splits exercises into two categories: **compound movements** (multi-joint exercises like bench press, squats, rows) and **isolation movements** (single-joint exercises like curls, lateral raises, leg extensions).

| Goal | Compound Reps | Isolation Reps | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build Muscle | 6-10 | 10-15 | The sweet spot for muscle growth (hypertrophy). Enough weight to challenge you, enough reps to create metabolic stress. |
| Build Strength | 3-6 | 6-10 | Heavy weight, fewer reps. Trains your nervous system to recruit more muscle fibers and produce maximum force. |
| Lose Fat | 8-12 | 12-18 | Higher reps keep your heart rate up and burn more calories while still preserving muscle. |
| Improve Fitness | 8-15 | 12-20 | Balanced ranges that improve both strength and endurance. Good for general health. |
| Sport & Conditioning | 8-15 | 12-20 | Similar to fitness — builds work capacity and power transfer for athletic performance. |

### Split Type

Your split type determines which muscle groups get trained on which days. The Foundry supports four split types, each with different day counts.

**Push/Pull/Legs (PPL)** — 3, 5, or 6 days per week

| Day | Muscles |
|---|---|
| Push | Chest, Shoulders, Triceps |
| Pull | Back (Lats), Biceps, Rear Delts |
| Legs | Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves |

With 3 days you do each once. With 6 days you do each twice, hitting every muscle group twice per week (which research shows is optimal for growth).

**Upper/Lower** — 2 or 4 days per week

| Day | Muscles |
|---|---|
| Upper | Chest, Back, Shoulders, Biceps, Triceps |
| Lower | Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves |

**Full Body** — 2 or 3 days per week

Every session trains the entire body. Good for beginners or people with limited gym time.

**Push/Pull** — 4 days per week

| Day | Muscles |
|---|---|
| Push | Chest, Shoulders, Quads, Triceps |
| Pull | Back, Hamstrings, Glutes, Biceps |

## How Each Workout Is Built

### Anchor Exercises

Every training day starts with one **anchor exercise**. This is the most important lift of the day — a heavy compound movement that you'll track progress on across your entire mesocycle.

Think of it like this: if it's Push Day, your anchor might be Bench Press. Everything else that day supports it.

The anchor is always listed first, gets the most warm-up attention, and is the exercise The Foundry uses to detect PRs and stalls.

### Accessory Exercises

After the anchor, The Foundry fills in accessory exercises based on:
1. Your session duration (shorter sessions = fewer exercises)
2. Which muscle groups need to be hit that day
3. What equipment you have available

| Session Duration | Total Exercises |
|---|---|
| 30 min or less | 3 |
| 31-45 min | 4 |
| 46-60 min | 5 |
| 61-75 min | 6 |
| 76+ min | 7 |

Accessories are paired with the day's target muscles. For example, on Pull Day, the app prioritizes exercises that hit Lats, Back, Shoulders, Biceps, and Upper Traps — in that order.

### Supersets

When The Foundry (or the AI) builds your program, it pairs complementary exercises as **supersets** — two exercises done back-to-back with no rest between them. Classic pairings include bench press + row, curls + tricep pushdowns, or leg extensions + leg curls. Only accessory exercises get paired — anchor lifts always stand alone.

## How Volume Progresses Through a Mesocycle

A mesocycle (meso) is a multi-week training block, typically 4 to 8 weeks. The Foundry uses a concept called **linear periodization** — volume (number of sets) increases over time, then drops for recovery.

### The Three Volume Phases

Each meso is split into three phases:

| Phase | When | Sets | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MEV** (Minimum Effective Volume) | First third of the meso | Base sets minus 1 | Ease in. Establish weights. Let your body adapt. |
| **MAV** (Maximum Adaptive Volume) | Middle third | Base sets (as prescribed) | The growth zone. This is where most gains happen. |
| **MRV** (Maximum Recoverable Volume) | Last third before deload | Base sets plus 1 | Push your limits. Maximum stimulus before recovery. |
| **Deload** | Final week | Always 2 sets | Recover. Light weights, low volume. Prepare for the next meso. |

**Example:** If an exercise is prescribed at 4 sets:
- Weeks 1-2 (MEV): 3 sets
- Weeks 3-4 (MAV): 4 sets
- Weeks 5 (MRV): 5 sets
- Week 6 (Deload): 2 sets

### Volume Landmarks by Muscle Group

The Foundry uses research-backed volume landmarks to ensure you're training enough but not too much:

| Muscle | MEV (minimum) | MAV Sweet Spot | MRV (max) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chest | 8 sets/week | 10-20 | 22 |
| Back | 10 | 14-22 | 25 |
| Shoulders | 6 | 8-16 | 20 |
| Quads | 8 | 10-20 | 25 |
| Hamstrings | 6 | 8-16 | 20 |
| Glutes | 4 | 6-16 | 20 |
| Biceps | 6 | 8-14 | 20 |
| Triceps | 4 | 6-14 | 18 |
| Calves | 6 | 8-16 | 20 |

## How Weight Suggestions Work (Carryover)

Each week, The Foundry looks at what you did last week and suggests what to do this week. This is called **carryover**.

The rule is simple: **if you hit all your prescribed reps on every working set last week, you earned a weight increase.**

The size of the increase depends on the equipment:

| Equipment | Weight Increase | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Barbell | +5 lbs | Standard 2.5 lb plates on each side |
| Dumbbell (under 25 lbs) | +2.5 lbs | Small dumbbells go up in 2.5 lb increments |
| Dumbbell (25+ lbs) | +5 lbs | Heavier dumbbells typically jump by 5 lbs |
| Cable/Machine (experienced) | +2.5 lbs | Advanced lifters need smaller jumps |
| Cable/Machine (others) | +5 lbs | Beginners/intermediates can handle bigger jumps |
| Bodyweight | No increase | You add reps instead |

**If you didn't hit all your reps**, the app suggests the same weight but adds one rep. This is called **rep progression** — you build up reps until you can handle a weight increase.

## The Readiness System

Before each workout, The Foundry asks you three questions:

1. **How did you sleep?** (Poor / OK / Good)
2. **How sore are you?** (High / Moderate / Low)
3. **How's your energy?** (Low / Moderate / High)

Each answer gets a score from 0 to 2. The three scores are added together for a total readiness score from 0 to 6.

| Score | Label | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| 5-6 | READY | All systems go. Train as planned. |
| 3-4 | MODERATE | Watch intensity. Push where it feels right, back off where it doesn't. |
| 0-2 | LOW | Recovery comes first. Consider reducing your weights by 10-15% today. |

The readiness score also feeds into stall detection. If your average readiness over the past week is 2.5 or lower, The Foundry flags potential stalls as fatigue-related rather than strength plateaus — which changes the advice it gives you.

## How Stall Detection Works

The Foundry watches your performance across weeks. If it notices you're not making progress, it tells you.

### Three-Week Plateau

If you've used the **exact same weight** for the same exercise across 3 consecutive weeks, The Foundry flags it as a stall. But it checks one thing first: if your current week already shows a heavier weight, it cancels the stall alert (you already broke through).

### Regression

If your weight went **down** compared to last week, that's flagged as a regression. This is more urgent than a plateau — something is off.

### Fatigue vs. True Stall

When a stall is detected, The Foundry checks your recent readiness scores. If your average readiness over the past 7 days is 2.5 or lower, it labels the stall as a **fatigue signal** — meaning the fix is more rest, not more effort.

### Special Case: Lose Fat Goal

If your goal is "Lose Fat" and a stall is detected, The Foundry also checks your body weight log. If your weight has been going down during the stall period, it labels this as **"protecting strength during a cut"** — which is actually a good sign. Maintaining the same weight on the bar while losing body weight means you're getting stronger relative to your size.

## How PRs (Personal Records) Are Detected

After each workout session, The Foundry scans your logged sets and compares them to every previous week in the current meso.

For each exercise, it finds your **best weight** (heaviest weight where you completed at least one rep) from previous weeks, and compares it to your best weight today. If today is higher — that's a PR.

The app also scans extra sessions (ad-hoc workouts outside the normal schedule) to make sure it doesn't miss any historical bests.

## How AI Program Generation Works

When you choose "Auto Build" in the setup flow, The Foundry sends your profile to Claude (Anthropic's AI) to design a custom mesocycle.

Here's exactly what gets sent to the AI:

- Your name
- Experience level (beginner/intermediate/advanced)
- Goal (build muscle, strength, lose fat, etc.)
- Split type and number of days
- Available equipment
- Session duration
- The full exercise database (300+ exercises with their IDs, tags, muscles, and difficulty)
- If this is your second meso or beyond: your previous meso's readiness data (average score, low-readiness days, recovery capacity assessment)

The AI is told to act as an "elite personal trainer with 40 years of experience" and designs the complete program following these rules:
- Start each day with one anchor (compound) lift
- Balance muscle groups across the week
- Use progressive overload principles
- Suggest antagonist superset pairings
- Match exercises to your available equipment

The AI returns a complete JSON structure with every day, exercise, set count, rep range, and coaching notes. The Foundry then clamps the AI's suggestions to experience-appropriate ranges (so a beginner doesn't get 6 sets of heavy deadlifts) and stores the result.

### What Makes the AI "Smart" Across Mesos

When you finish a meso and start a new one, The Foundry tells the AI about your recovery profile from the previous meso:

- How many readiness check-ins you logged
- Your average readiness score
- How many low-readiness days you had

Based on this, it adjusts the new program:
- **Low recovery capacity (avg ≤ 2.5)**: "Keep weeks 1-2 conservative — fewer sets, higher RIR. Let fatigue clear before ramping."
- **Moderate recovery (2.5-3.5)**: "Standard volume ramp. Watch for fatigue in peak weeks."
- **Good recovery (3.5+)**: "Standard or slightly aggressive volume ramp is appropriate."

## How Meso Archiving Works

When a mesocycle ends (either completed or manually reset), The Foundry archives it:

1. **Saves the full profile snapshot** — your settings at the time
2. **Records anchor peak weights** — the heaviest weight you hit on each anchor exercise
3. **Calculates readiness summary** — average score, total check-ins, low-readiness day count
4. **Stores the archive** — prepended to your archive list (newest first)
5. **Enforces the 11-meso cap** — keeps 1 active + 10 archived. The oldest archive gets dropped if you exceed this.

This archived data feeds into the next meso's AI program generation, creating a feedback loop where each training block is informed by the last.

## The Warmup Protocol

The Foundry generates specific warmup instructions based on the exercise and your working weight.

For **barbell anchor exercises** (your main lift of the day), the full warmup protocol is:

| Step | Load | Reps | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bar only | 10 | Neural activation — feel the groove, zero load |
| 2 | 50% of working weight | 5 | Dial in bar path. Controlled tempo. |
| 3 | 70% of working weight | 3 | Approaching working weight. Stay tight. |
| 4 | 85% of working weight | 1 | Final primer — then load up and go. |

For **accessory exercises**, you get a simpler warmup: usually "1 feeler set" (one light set to feel the movement before going heavy).

If your session is 30 minutes or shorter, anchor warmups are condensed to "2 ramp sets" to save time.

## Summary: The Foundry Decision Tree

```
User signs up
  → Sets experience level → Filters exercise database by difficulty
  → Sets goal → Determines rep ranges for all exercises
  → Sets split type → Determines which muscles train on which days
  → Sets equipment → Filters exercises to what they can use
  → Sets session duration → Determines exercises per day (3-7)

Program generates (AI or manual)
  → Each day gets 1 anchor (compound) + accessories
  → Supersets paired where appropriate

Each week of the meso
  → Week 1-2 (MEV): Base sets minus 1
  → Week 3-4 (MAV): Base sets as prescribed
  → Week 5+ (MRV): Base sets plus 1
  → Final week (Deload): 2 sets across the board

Before each workout
  → Readiness check: Sleep + Soreness + Energy = Score (0-6)
  → Score determines training advice for the day

During the workout
  → Previous week's weights carry over with progression suggestions
  → Hit all reps? Weight goes up. Didn't? Add a rep.

After the workout
  → PR detection: Compare today's best to all previous weeks
  → Stall detection: Same weight 3 weeks in a row?
    → Check readiness: Is it fatigue or a real plateau?
    → Check body weight: If cutting, are you protecting strength?

Meso ends
  → Archive: Save peaks, readiness summary, profile snapshot
  → Feed into next meso's AI program generation
  → The cycle continues, each meso building on the last
```
